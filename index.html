Bio

I am a research assistant working at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction, digital health, wearable systems, explainable AI, and design. My research explores how complex health, behavioral, and physiological data can be translated into understandable, embodied, and reflective interaction systems.

My background is in product design and interaction design. I received my undergraduate degree in Product and Automotive Design from Shandong University of Art & Design, and my master's degree in Product Design from Nihon University College of Art. During my master's study, I also worked at Huawei Japan, where I gained experience in wearable and mobile interaction design.

My recent work focuses on health interaction systems, wearable sensing, human-food interaction, and digital fabrication. I am especially interested in designing technologies that help people understand body-related data and reflect on everyday health and well-being.

Selected Projects

DIIve: Inflammation-Aware Dietary Feedback

DIIve is a human-food interaction system that translates inflammation-aware dietary analysis into reflective feedback about food choices, body experiences, and subjective well-being. The project explores how abstract dietary signals can become more understandable and actionable in everyday life.

KiriInflate: Soft Inflatable Interfaces

KiriInflate explores soft inflatable structures for tangible interaction. My work contributed to the development of soft inflatable prototypes and application examples, including wearable assistive interfaces and interactive objects.

Wearable Muscle Sensing for Exoskeleton Assistance

This project investigates human-in-the-loop wearable sensing for predictive and adaptive exoskeleton assistance. It connects wearable sensing, human movement, and adaptive feedback for assistive robotics.

NutriLoop and ChronoBite

These projects explore how dietary structure, inflammation-related analysis, and cellular-aging-related concepts can be translated into interactive feedback systems for health reflection and everyday dietary decision-making.
